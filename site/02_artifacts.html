Lifecycle Artifacts#

This section encodes a minimal IEC 61508 SIL 2 lifecycle thread for an Emergency Stop function as structured Sphinx-Needs artifacts.

Hazard#

Hazard: Emergency Stop does not remove hazardous energy HAZ_ESTOP_01
phase: Hazard and Risk Analysis
owner_role: Safety Engineer
part_ref: IEC 61508-1 (Hazard and Risk Analysis)
addressed_by: RISK_ESTOP_01 [Risk Assessment]

If the Emergency Stop request is not effective, the machine may continue hazardous motion, exposing personnel to injury.

Risk Assessment#

Risk Assessment: Risk of injury due to ineffective Emergency Stop RISK_ESTOP_01
phase: Hazard and Risk Analysis
owner_role: Safety Engineer
part_ref: IEC 61508-1 (Risk Assessment)
addresses: HAZ_ESTOP_01 [Hazard]
allocated_by: SIL_ESTOP_01 [SIL Allocation]

Risk evaluation indicates that risk reduction is required for the Emergency Stop function.

SIL Allocation#

SIL Allocation: SIL 2 allocated for Emergency Stop safety function SIL_ESTOP_01
phase: Safety Function Allocation
sil_level: SIL2
owner_role: Functional Safety Manager
part_ref: IEC 61508-1 (SIL Allocation)
allocates: RISK_ESTOP_01 [Risk Assessment]
refined_by: FSR_ESTOP_01 [Functional Safety Requirement]
reviewed_by: CONF_ESTOP_01 [Confirmation Measure]

Based on the risk assessment, the Emergency Stop safety function is allocated SIL 2.

Functional Safety Requirement#

Functional Safety Requirement: Emergency Stop shall de-energize motor outputs within 100 ms FSR_ESTOP_01
phase: Safety Requirements Specification
sil_level: SIL2
owner_role: System Engineer
part_ref: IEC 61508-2 (SRS)
refines: SIL_ESTOP_01 [SIL Allocation]
refined_by: TSR_ESTOP_01 [Technical Safety Requirement]
verified_by: VERIF_ESTOP_01 [Verification Activity]

When the Emergency Stop is activated, the system shall remove power from motor outputs within 100 ms.

Technical Safety Requirement#

Technical Safety Requirement: Dual-channel Emergency Stop input with discrepancy detection TSR_ESTOP_01
phase: Safety Requirements Specification
sil_level: SIL2
owner_role: Hardware Engineer
part_ref: IEC 61508-2 (SRS)
refines: FSR_ESTOP_01 [Functional Safety Requirement]
realized_by: ARCH_ESTOP_01 [Safety Architecture]

The Emergency Stop shall be implemented with two independent input channels and discrepancy detection to detect wiring or contact faults.

Safety Architecture#

Safety Architecture: 1oo2 input evaluation and safety relay output stage ARCH_ESTOP_01
phase: Architectural Design
owner_role: System Architect
part_ref: IEC 61508-2 (Architecture)
realizes: TSR_ESTOP_01 [Technical Safety Requirement]

The architecture evaluates both input channels and commands a safety relay / safe output stage to remove motor power.

Verification Activity#

Verification Activity: Measure Emergency Stop response time VERIF_ESTOP_01
phase: Verification
owner_role: Test Engineer
part_ref: IEC 61508-3 (Verification)
verifies: FSR_ESTOP_01 [Functional Safety Requirement]
evidenced_by: EVID_ESTOP_01 [Evidence Artifact]

Verification confirms the response time from Emergency Stop activation to output de-energization.

Evidence#

Evidence Artifact: Emergency Stop response time test report EVID_ESTOP_01
phase: Verification Evidence
owner_role: Test Engineer
artifact_kind: Test Report
evidences: VERIF_ESTOP_01 [Verification Activity]

Evidence artifact documenting the measured response time and acceptance criteria.

Confirmation Measure#

Confirmation Measure: Independent review of SIL allocation and safety requirements CONF_ESTOP_01
phase: Functional Safety Assessment
owner_role: Independent Assessor
part_ref: IEC 61508-1 (Confirmation Measures)
reviews: SIL_ESTOP_01 [SIL Allocation]

Confirmation activity records independent review of the SIL allocation decision and the derived safety requirements.
